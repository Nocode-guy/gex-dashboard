Understanding Gamma Exposure for Smarter Trading Decisions

‚ö° What is GEX?

Gamma Exposure (GEX) measures how much market makers must buy or sell to hedge their options positions as price moves.

Key Concept

When price approaches a high GEX strike, market makers' hedging activity creates price magnetism or resistance.

Positive GEX Price Magnet
Negative GEX Price Accelerator

üëë Key Zones Explained

Zone Meaning Trading Action
KING Highest +GEX strike Strong magnet, price drawn here
GATEKEEPER Highest -GEX strike Accelerator zone, fast moves
ZERO GAMMA GEX flips sign Below = volatile, Above = stable
Pro Tip: When price is between King and Zero Gamma, expect range-bound action. Below Zero Gamma, expect trending moves.

üìà VEX & DEX

Vanna Exposure (VEX) - Sensitivity to implied volatility changes

  • High VEX = IV changes cause big delta hedging
  • Watch during earnings, FOMC, CPI

Delta Exposure (DEX) - Raw directional exposure

  • Shows where directional bets are concentrated
  • High DEX = potential support/resistance
Use VEX when: Expecting volatility events. VEX shows where IV crush/expansion will have biggest impact.

üéØ Quick Trading Strategies

1. Mean Reversion (Above Zero Gamma)

Fade moves away from King strike. Price tends to return to high +GEX areas.

2. Trend Following (Below Zero Gamma)

Trade with momentum. -GEX zones accelerate moves, don't fight the trend.

3. Level Trading

Use King as target, Gatekeeper as stop zone. High GEX strikes act as S/R.

üìã The 5 Rules of GEX Trading

1. Respect the King

Price gravitates to highest +GEX. Use as profit target.

2. Fear the Gatekeeper

Highest -GEX accelerates moves. Set stops beyond it.

3. Know Your Zone

Above Zero Gamma = range. Below = trend.

4. Watch 0DTE

Same-day expiration has strongest gamma effect.

5. Flow Confirms

Use options flow to confirm GEX levels.
